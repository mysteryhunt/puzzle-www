Resources for Beginners

If you are just starting as a solver in the MIT Mystery Hunt, some of the resources below may prove useful. We have included some readings on the general art of puzzle solving, and links to a few selected puzzles that you can use for practice.

  • Beginner's Guide, from Mission Street
  • Puzzling Basics, from Puzzled Pint
  • Introduction to Puzzlehunts, by Brian Chen
  • Left Out recorded their 2020 How to Hunt Workshop:

    • Part 2: Sample Puzzle
    • Part 3: Sample Puzzle cont’d
    • Part 4: Meta Puzzles
    • Part 5: Tech Tools
    • Part 6: Tech Tools cont’d
  • How to Hunt Workshop from One Fish, Two Fish, Random Fish, Blue Fish in 2016

  • Thinking About Puzzles, by Allen Rabinovich
  • The Atlantic's Guide to Solving Cryptic Clues, The Atlantic Monthly
  • Sarcone and Waeber's Rules to Puzzle Solving, Archimedes-lab.org
  • betaveros's "Have You Tried"
  • teammate Hunt's "Have You Tried"
